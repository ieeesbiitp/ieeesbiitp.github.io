About

NSRS 2023 is a common platform for graduate students to postdoctoral researchers, to present their research findings and discuss with peers and experts in their field.

This symposium will provide a platform for sharing knowledge, receiving feedback, and networking within the research community of various disciplines like electrical and electronics engineering, computer science and engineering, applied sciences, and interdisciplinary research.

The Research symposia will primarily be opened for masters, Ph.D. students, and researchers to share their research ideas through posters, whereas the TechnoTricks event is for any B.Tech, M.Tech, or PhD student to present their models and showcase their practicing engineering skills.

This event will provide a peer platform for collaborations with the experts in these fields. Exciting prizes with certificates for all participants are also offered in this program.

Plenary Talks
IEEE Day brings together some of the brightest minds and experts in the field to inspire and educate. Our speaker sessions are a highlight of the event, offering participants a unique opportunity to gain insights from industry leaders and visionaries.
Research Symposium
The IEEE Day Symposium is a cornerstone of our celebration, offering a platform for in-depth exploration of the latest technological advancements and research breakthroughs. This intellectual gathering serves as a melting pot of ideas, fostering collaboration and innovation.
TechnoTricks
"TechnoTricks" is an exciting and hands-on activity that lies at the heart of our IEEE Day celebration. This event challenges participants to apply their engineering skills, creativity, and problem-solving abilities to tackle real-world problems and come up with innovative solutions. The top 3 selected problem-based prototypes will get a chance to pre-incubate and develop full product.
